## **📌 Overview**

This matrix defines **hard skills, soft skills, domain knowledge, and tools** for **Research roles** (academic, industry, or applied), categorized by **proficiency levels** (Novice → Expert). Use it to:  
✅ Assess your current research skills.  
✅ Identify gaps for career growth (e.g., from Analyst to Strategist).  
✅ Map transferable skills to other domains (see [Transferability Matrix](../transferability.md)).

## **📊 Skill Level Definitions**


| **Level**      | **Description**                                 | **Example Tasks**                                                            | **Time to Achieve** |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| **Novice**     | Basic understanding; requires supervision.      | Summarize papers, assist with data collection, follow research protocols.    | 1–3 months          |
| **Analyst**    | Proficient in application; can troubleshoot.    | Design experiments, analyze data, draft research reports.                    | 6–12 months         |
| **Strategist** | Designs solutions; aligns with research goals.  | Develop research frameworks, lead studies, publish in mid-tier journals.     | 1–2 years           |
| **Engineer**   | Builds, deploys, and scales research solutions. | Automate data pipelines, build reproducibility systems, secure large grants. | 2–3 years           |
| **Expert**     | Innovates, pushes boundaries, mentors others.   | &nbsp;                                                                       | &nbsp;              |
