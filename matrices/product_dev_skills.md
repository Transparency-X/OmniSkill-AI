## **📌 Overview**

This matrix defines **hard skills, soft skills, domain knowledge, and tools** for **Product Development roles**, categorized by **proficiency levels** (Novice → Expert). Use it to:  
✅ Assess your current product management and development skills.  
✅ Identify gaps for career growth (e.g., from Analyst to Strategist).  
✅ Map transferable skills to other domains (see [Transferability Matrix](../transferability.md)).

## **📊 Skill Level Definitions**


| **Level**      | **Description**                                | **Example Tasks**                                                                           | **Time to Achieve** |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| **Novice**     | Basic understanding; requires supervision.     | Write user stories, assist with sprints, conduct basic user interviews.                     | 1–3 months          |
| **Analyst**    | Proficient in application; can troubleshoot.   | Prioritize features, analyze user feedback, create roadmaps.                                | 6–12 months         |
| **Strategist** | Designs solutions; aligns with business goals. | Define product vision, design GTM strategies, align roadmaps with business objectives.      | 1–2 years           |

## **🎯 Example Roadmaps**

### **Path 1: Novice → Analyst (6–12 Months)**

1. **Months 1–3**:
  - Learn **Agile/Scrum** (Scrum Alliance, [Atlassian’s Guide](https://www.atlassian.com/agile)).
  - Complete a **product management course** (e.g., [Reforge](https://www.reforge.com/)).
  - Assist with **user interviews** and **roadmap updates**.
2. **Months 4–6**:
  - Lead a **small feature** from ideation to launch.
  - Use **Jira/Productboard** to manage backlogs.
  - Analyze **user feedback** and **A/B test results**.
3. **Months 7–12**:
  - Design a **quarterly roadmap** for a product area.
  - Conduct **competitor analysis** and **market research**.
  - Earn a certification (e.g., [Pragmatic Institute](https://www.pragmaticinstitute.com/)).

### **Path 2: Analyst → Strategist (1–2 Years)**

1. **Year 1**:
  - Lead **user research** for a major feature.
  - Develop a **GTM strategy** for a product launch.
  - Study **business models** (e.g., [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/)).
2. **Year 2**:
  - Define the **product vision** for a new initiative.
  - Publish a **product strategy doc**.
  - Mentor a **junior product manager**.

### **Path 3: Strategist → Engineer (2–3 Years)**

1. **Year 1**:
  - Scale **Agile/Scrum** across multiple teams.
  - Design **custom prioritization frameworks**.
  - Lead **cross-functional product initiatives**.
2. **Year 2**:
  - Build **prototyping tools** for the team.
  - Optimize **data pipelines** for product insights.
  - Speak at **product conferences** (e.g., INDUSTRY, Mind the Product).
3. **Year 3**:
  - Create **enterprise product platforms**.
  - Shape **industry product standards**.
  - Publish **thought leadership** on product development.
